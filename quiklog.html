Radiant QuikLog

One inventory for every linac, CT simulator, HDR unit, ion chamber, electrometer, well chamber, and survey meter in your department — with calibration due-dates, configurable warnings, and audit-ready printable reports.

Users Guide Request a Demo

Evaluation / testing use only — not a medical device. Radiant QuikLog is a record-keeping aid. The cal-due tolerances and warning thresholds it surfaces must be reviewed and approved by a Qualified Medical Physicist or Radiation Safety Officer for your institution. Do not transmit PHI through this software.

Features

What QuikLog does.

An equipment master that runs on the QA workstation — every asset, every cert, every cal-due date in one place.

Equipment Master

One inventory across linacs, CT sims, HDR units, ion chambers, electrometers, well chambers, survey meters, and custom equipment types — with manufacturer, model, S/N, location, and date of first use.

Calibration Schedule

Per-asset last-cal and next-due dates, with a configurable cal interval. The schedule view sorts by what's due first and surfaces overdue items at the top.

Due-Soon Warnings

Color-coded status — OK, Due Soon, Overdue, No Date — with a configurable lead time (default 60 days). No more surprise overdue surveys.

Calibration History

Every calibration event recorded against an asset — cal date, next-due, performed by, cert / ADCL number, and notes. Full audit trail per piece of equipment.

Printable Reports

Full inventory PDF and a filtered "due in next N days" PDF for surveys, JCAHO visits, and annual physics reviews — cleanly formatted and ready for the binder.

Local-First

SQLite on the QA workstation — no cloud, no IT involvement. Same engine powers the standalone Windows app and the local web app, so the data lives where you work.

Who It's For

Made for QA leads & RSOs.

QuikLog is built for the clinical medical physicist, QA lead, or Radiation Safety Officer who owns the equipment master — the spreadsheet that's always almost up to date. It replaces that spreadsheet with a structured record that answers the questions surveys and annual reviews actually ask: what's in service, what was last calibrated, what's due next month, and where's the cert?

It runs as either a standalone Windows app or a local web app on the QA workstation, with a single SQLite database shared between them — nothing leaves the machine. The schedule view, due-soon coloring, and inventory reports are the same in both UIs.
